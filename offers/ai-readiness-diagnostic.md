<div class="eyebrow">What's assessed</div>

- Operating model: how work actually gets done day to day, compared with what the org chart says happens
- Governance: who really owns AI decisions, and whether that's written down anywhere or just assumed
- Data quality: can what's feeding your AI systems be trusted, and where has it quietly come apart
- Decision rights: where decisions get made, how fast, and what happens when one of them turns out wrong
- Leadership and psychological safety: do people raise concerns about an AI output, or stay quiet and let it through
- Delivery maturity: the technical foundations underneath, and whether they can carry what's being asked of them

<div class="eyebrow">How it works</div>

<ol class="offer-steps">
  <li>A scoping call confirms which stakeholders and which parts of the business are in scope.</li>
  <li>Structured data gathering across all six domains, combining interviews, document review and a look at the artefacts that show how work actually happens.</li>
  <li>Findings are scored and cross-checked against the patterns behind each domain.</li>
  <li>You receive the heat map and sit through a structured debrief to walk through what it means and what to do about it.</li>
</ol>

<div class="eyebrow">The output</div>

A heat map ranking risk across all six domains, supported by a written narrative explaining what's driving each score, delivered as a report and walked through in a structured debrief. No generic AI maturity model, no vendor shortlist, nothing assembled from a template.

<div class="eyebrow">What this isn't</div>

Strategy work. There's no tool selection and no roadmap design. What you're paying for is a straight answer on where the organisation isn't ready for what's being asked of it, and why.
